Systemd impressions
I recently decided to get a new, fresh Arch Linux install. I'd customize it from the ground up to be as functional and fast as possible, adhering to the KISS principle, yet satisfying all my needs and having a little bit eye candy. Seeing as SysV Init is considered to be a major factor in bootup speed, I decided to look at the most obvious drop-in replacement, systemd. After setting up the basic Arch root system, I installed and configured it. The only real pain was setting up the static network without any network manager, but with a little fiddling I managed that too.

Now, systemd does not only provide the init process, but it handles things like session management and logging too. So syslog-ng (or any other logging daemon) is not necessary anymore. There is one big disadvantage to systemd's journal, it doesn't store its logs in plain text. Posted on September 18th, 2012 by Florian Dejonckheere - no comments
